The Two Choices: Scale vs. Speed

Architecture A: ZeroETL with Redshift

"Higher cost, scales infinitely. Best for complex, near real-time analytics."

  • Best For: > 2 TB Data
  • Data Latency: 1-5 Minutes
  • Scalability: 500+ Concurrent Users
Key Use Case:

Complex business intelligence (BI), data science, and training predictive AI/ML models on massive historical datasets.

Architecture B: The Read Replica

"Lower cost, simpler operations. Best for real-time operational dashboards."

  • Best For: < 1 TB Data
  • Data Latency: < 1 Second (Real-Time)
  • Scalability: 100-200 Concurrent Users
Key Use Case:

Live operational dashboards for sales, marketing, or logistics teams who need to see data as it happens.

The Key Insight: The Break-Even Point

The Read Replica (Arch. B) is cheaper for smaller datasets, but the ZeroETL (Arch. A) scales more cost-effectively. The lines cross at 2,000 GB.

Which Scenario Fits Your Business?

Small-Medium Client

Businesses with data volumes < 1 TB who need real-time operational dashboards for a core team.

Recommendation:

Architecture B (Read Replica)

Prioritizes real-time speed and lower cost.

Large Enterprise Client

Organizations managing data > 2 TB with 500+ users and a need for complex, large-scale AI/ML.

Recommendation:

Architecture A (ZeroETL)

Built for massive scale and advanced analytics.

What About Region?

"For cost, it doesn't matter."

The dashboard confirms identical pricing between major US regions. Your regional decision should be based on:

  • Latency: Choose the region closest to your users for the fastest dashboard performance.
  • Newest Services: US-East-1 (N. Virginia) typically gets new AWS services and features first.
